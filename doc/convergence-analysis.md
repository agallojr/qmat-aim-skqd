# Convergence Analysis: Ce 4f Stretch Case (20 qubits)

## System

- **Element**: Cerium 4f (J=5/2 multiplet, 5 orbitals)
- **Model**: Multi-orbital Anderson Impurity Model, Kanamori interactions
- **Geometry**: 5 imp × 1 bath = 10 spatial orbitals = 20 qubits
- **Hilbert dim**: C(10,5)² = 63,504
- **Parameters**: U=6.0, U'=4.6, J_H=0.7, μ=3.0, V=0.4 eV
- **Crystal field**: ε = [-0.15, -0.05, 0.0, 0.05, 0.15] eV

## Observed Convergence Floor

| Krylov dim | Shots     | Error (%) | Error (meV) | Notes                     |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|---------------------------|
| 4         | sv (10k)  | 93.3      | 2684        | Severe: sv samples too few |
| 4–10      | 65k       | 15.6      | 450         | Shot-saturated plateau     |
| 4–10      | 262k      | 15.6      | 450         | Confirmed: not shot-limited|
| 4–10      | 1M        | 15.6      | 450         | Same                       |
| 12        | 65k       | 44.9      | 1292        | Worse: time-evolved states overshoot |

**Key finding**: Error plateaus at 15.6% regardless of shot count (65k–1M) or
Krylov dimension (4–10). The floor is **ansatz-limited**, not sampling-limited.

## Root Cause

The first-order Trotter decomposition of e^{-iHt} has limited fidelity for this
strongly correlated system. At U/V = 15 (deep Mott regime), the ground state is
highly multi-reference — the Krylov states generated by first-order Trotter
simply do not span the subspace containing the true ground state.

Increasing k beyond 6 is counterproductive: deeper time evolutions produce states
that oscillate away from the ground state, degrading SQD subspace quality.

## Paths Below 15%

### 1. Second-order Trotter (recommended, localized change)

Replace the first-order product formula:

    e^{-iHdt} ≈ e^{-iH₁dt} e^{-iH₂dt} ...

with the symmetric (Suzuki) decomposition:

    e^{-iHdt} ≈ e^{-iH₁dt/2} e^{-iH₂dt/2} ... e^{-iH₂dt/2} e^{-iH₁dt/2}

This halves the Trotter error for a ~2× increase in circuit depth per step.
Implementation: modify `step2_krylov.py` to accept `--trotter-order {1,2}`.

### 2. Adaptive time-step schedule

Instead of uniform dt spacing (t, 2t, 3t, ...), use a geometric or
optimized schedule. Some time steps produce much better ground-state overlap.
Requires empirical tuning or a classical pre-screening step.

### 3. Better reference state

Currently |ψ₀⟩ = Hartree-Fock (single Slater determinant). For a 5-orbital
f-electron system deep in the Mott regime, this has poor overlap with the
correlated ground state. Options:
- CASCI reference from a cheap classical calculation
- DMRG-informed initial state
- Symmetry-adapted linear combination of determinants

### 4. Variational pre-optimization

Replace or augment Krylov with a shallow variational ansatz (UCCSD, HEA)
that's been coarsely optimized to maximize ground-state overlap before
SQD post-processing refines the subspace.

### 5. Increase SQD subspace budget

At 63,504-dim Hilbert space, the current 800 samples/batch may not capture
all relevant determinants. Increasing to 2000+ could help marginally, though
this is unlikely to break through the ansatz floor alone.

### 6. Reduce correlation strength (changes physics)

Increasing V from 0.4 to 0.6+ eV (stronger hybridization) makes the ground
state less multi-reference and more accessible to the Trotter ansatz. This
changes the physics but may be appropriate for different host materials.

## Recommendation

**Second-order Trotter** is the cleanest next step:
- Localized to `step2_krylov.py`
- No change to SQD internals or physics
- Expected to roughly halve the ansatz error
- Adds `--trotter-order` CLI flag with backward compatibility

This is a separate task from the current metadata/plotting enrichment work.
